About

Enrolled as an Advocate in 1998, I began my career under the guidance of Senior Advocate Mr. G. Papi Reddy. I pursued my Law degree and Masters in Law from University Law College, Bengaluru. I practice before Family Courts, Civil Courts, the High Court and Tribunals. I am a Standing Counsel for Public Sector Companies and also represent Life Insurance Companies. Alongside full-time practice, I actively offer pro bono services to the poor and underprivileged. I am also a trained Mediator of the Karnataka Mediation Centre and believe in dignified, time-saving dispute resolution.

Vision for the Bar

A Bar where every advocate‚Äîyoung or senior, urban or district, woman or first-generation‚Äîfeels protected, respected, and empowered. Welfare should be accessible and time-bound; professional dignity must be defended; mentorship should be structured; and mediation should be strengthened so clients get faster, fair outcomes without compromising rights.

Manifesto

1) Advocate Welfare that Works

  • Time-bound, transparent processing for welfare-related benefits with clear tracking and guidance.
  • Single-window assistance for bereaved families: checklist-based support for nominee/widow claims.
  • District-level facilitation camps to reduce repeated visits and time loss.

2) Young Advocates & Mentorship

  • Monthly clinics: drafting discipline, evidence basics, court craft, and practical case-management.
  • Mentor network with seniors/standing counsel to guide first-generation advocates.
  • Helpdesk for essential updates, procedural guidance, and professional support.

3) Women Advocates: Safety + Leadership

  • Confidential, time-bound support pathway for harassment/intimidation concerns.
  • Meaningful representation in committees, panels, and training initiatives.
  • Stronger coordination for basic facilities and safe access in court premises.

4) Faster Justice through Mediation

  • Mediation readiness: negotiation skills + settlement drafting that protects client interest.
  • Strengthen court-annexed mediation usage with awareness and professional accountability.
  • Dignified dispute resolution‚Äîespecially in family and civil matters‚Äîwithout coercion.

5) Pro Bono with Impact

  • Structured pro bono roster and legal literacy clinics for the underprivileged.
  • Support access to justice with dignity, clarity, and consistent follow-up.

6) Professional Dignity & Ethics

  • Promote professional discipline, courteous court conduct, and transparent client communication.
  • Defend advocates from unfair intimidation and strengthen institutional respect for the Bar.

100-Day Deliverables

  1. Launch welfare guidance + tracking support (helpdesk format) and district-level facilitation camps.
  2. Publish a mentorship calendar for young advocates (monthly practical sessions).
  3. Implement a women-advocate support pathway (confidential, time-bound response).
  4. Start ‚Äúmediation readiness‚Äù workshops for advocates: negotiation + settlement drafting.
  5. Run legal literacy + pro bono clinics focused on family rights, maintenance, and essential civil remedies.
